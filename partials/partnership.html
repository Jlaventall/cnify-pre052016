Partnership or WFOE?

In considering establishing a presence in China, many businesses are faced with the decision of establishing a partnership with an already established Chinese entity, or going alone as a Wholly-Foreign-Owned-Enterprise (WFOE). Both methods have their pro’s and con’s.

As a WFOE, you retain full control of your business and brand’s activities in China. To achieve this status, you must become registered and dedicate a sometimes substantial capital investment to the enterprise. In almost all cases you will need the expertise of legal professionals who are well-versed in Chinese business law to properly advise you of your best options.

As a partnership, you trade control for in-market domain knowledge and expertise. Partnering with a Chinese entity can be a good experience if there’s sufficient amount of trust built between partners. This rarely, if ever, happens over night. It may take several visits in person and a framework to build a working relationship that is sufficient to support a working partnership.
